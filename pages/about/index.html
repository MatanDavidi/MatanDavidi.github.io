I attended primary school in Cassarate, and immediately afterwards middle school in Lugano 1.

Given my keen interest in information technology from an early age (see the "Personal history" section), I decided not to take a high school diploma, but rather to start studying information technology at the Vocational secondary school of Trevano-Canobbio, where I learned to programme for desktops (Java and C#), for the web (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP), manage data and databases (SQL), configure company servers (AD, LDAP, DHCP, DNS, group policies, etc. ), configure corporate networks, and above all design and implement individual and group projects.

In addition, in my spare time I learned to take my first steps with Python (e.g. GoogleTranslateDestroyer, where I used Django and mouuff / mtranslate) and Unreal Engine (see Cameriere).

Studying computer science for the first time was an amazing experience! It opened up a whole new world of knowledge and possibilities that I hadn't been aware of before, and I was so happy to be learning something I was passionate about! But it was more than that. I was beginning to see the actual scope of the field, and it made me want to know more. Much more! I realised that just studying computer science would not be enough for me: I wanted to know as much about it as humanely possible, so I set my sights on the best institution I could realistically enrol in: ETH Zürich.

After graduating from the vocational secondary school with the Diploma of Vocational Education and Training (what in German would be known as an EFZ, or "Eidgenössisches Fähigkeitszeugnis") and the Technical Vocational Baccalaureate ("Berufsmatura" in German), however, one last obstacle appeared before the enrolment. As I had graduated from a technical secondary school and not a regular high school, I could not enrol directly, and thus I would have either had to pass some entrance exams, or follow a one-year Bridge course ("Passerelle-Kurs" in German). I opted for the latter.

Finally, in 2021, I enrolled into the Computer Science bachelor course of study at ETH Zürich, and I was beyond excited. I found an apartment, packed my bags, and moved to the canton of Zürich, and was beside myself throughout the entire experience. Ask my parents: from day one I was ecstatic to be able to partake in the highest quality computer science education available in Switzerland, taught by professors who made significant contributions to the subject of their class (some of them even covering what they themselves discovered （ ๑✧∀✧๑ ）). Not without struggles and off-days, I worked diligently and always gave it my best, so even if I ended up failing, I'd have no regrets looking back, and three years later, as I'm writing this in August 2024, I am close enough to graduating that I can taste it, and it tastes like Rösti and Pastetli.

Thank you for reading ❤️

I was born at the age of -0.25 years in the city of Luzern (LU), Switzerland, and was transported back to Lugano (TI) a few months later, where I was raised.

Already from a very young age I found a deep interest in computers, with one of my earliest memory being helping my dad to build a computer for a customer (if by "helping" you mean holding the flashlight and staring in awe).

Shortly thereafter, I discovered the wonderful rabbithole that is ✨video games✨: Sonic Adventure, Pokémon FireRed, Super Mario 64, The Legend of Zelda, Banjo-Kazooie, The Sims, and so many more... I received a modest PC from my father, and I spent most of my free time on it (usually causing damage), and I have since discovered, especially getting older, that video games could not only entertain and waste time, but send a deeply powerful message, evoke emotions in the player, and create a lasting impact on them. Video games are not just another mindless activity, I found. They are a form of art.

Then, in my early teenage years, when being a YouTuber was slowly becoming a viable carreer choice, I was caught into the YouTube fame whirlwind. I started creating and editing audio, videos and images, and showing them to friends and family. This gave me plenty of hours of experience during which I have taught myself how to use all manners of editing software, including Adobe Premiere Pro and Adobe Photoshop. I continued for about 7 years, excited about the possibility to entertain myself and others by just doing what I loved. I was hooked on the possibility of creating very real ties with people from faraway places by just being myself.

After a percussions teacher visited us in elementary school, I became curious about music and rhythm. My father has always been the music lover in the family, and I grew up overhearing the countless genres and styles coming from his speakers, but at that point I saw that there was something special about music, so I asked my parents if I could take drumming lessons.

Let me tell you, the voyage I have been on since I started playing music led me to discover the joy that is music, not just as a listener, where of course you can form a tie by relating to the emotions that surround the artist once they step on stage, but also as a performer, where you can send out a figurative message in a bottle, and whoever relates to it can choose to stick around, and for a split second a very powerful link is formed. There are few better feelings.

And, in case you were wondering, I started learning music at 11 years of age, and I only stopped before moving to Zürich, when I was 20 years old. Playing however... That, I just can't bring myself to stop ;)

Finally, in the last few years of middle school, all these aspects converged, and the influence I received from that magical black box under my desk culminated into a passion for computer science: a discipline that would allow me to study how the PC, which brought me countless hours of fun and entertainment, actually functioned, and, even more importantly, how to use it to create something that could be useful or entertaining for others. I think computer science can be a form of art: it can help you fulfil a vision, share your story with the world, provide everyone with something you feel society is missing, and if people relate they can stick around.

And, since you're reading this, thank you for sticking around ❤️
